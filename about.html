About

I am a Scientific Data Developer at the BioInnovation Institute (BII) in Copenhagen, Denmark.

I am a bioinformatics scientist at Chalmers University of Technology in Gothenburg, Sweden, where I work as a member of NBIS (National Bioinformatics Infrastructure Sweden), which is a part of SciLifeLab (Science for Life Laboratory).

I was born in Burlington, Vermont, but grew up in Fort Collins, Colorado. I received a B.S. in Chemical & Biological Engineering and a B.S. in Chemistry from Colorado State University in 2010. During my time at Colorado State, I had the opportunity join a research group led by Arthur Mayeno and Brad Reisfeld. Working with them inspired me to pursue a graduate degree, and in 2016 I was awarded a Ph.D. in Chemical & Biological Engineering from Princeton University, under the supervision of Mark Brynildsen. Near the end of my Ph.D., I met Jens Nielsen during a visit he made to Princeton for a seminar. Soon after, I joined his group in Gothenburg, Sweden as a postdoc for four years.

At the start of 2020, I joined NBIS (National Bioinformatic Infrastructure Sweden), which is a part of SciLifeLab (Science for Life Laboratory), as a bioinformatics scientist. While at NBIS, I worked on projects with research groups throughout Sweden, in addition to teaching workshops and continuing to learn and develop new methods in computational biology and bioinformatics.

After a year at NBIS, I was offered a position at BII in Copenhagen, Denmark. As a Scientific Data Developer at BII, I am responsible for their data analysis and management infrastructure efforts, which aim to support early-stage life science start-up companies with heavy data or computation needs.

When I'm not in lab, I enjoy cycling, snowboarding, learning languages (Swedish and Finnish), coding, and traveling.
