About

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Division of Systems and Synthetic Biology (SysBio), which is part of the Biology and Biological Engineering department at Chalmers University of Technology in Gothenburg, Sweden.

I was born in Burlington, Vermont, but grew up in Fort Collins, Colorado. I received a B.S. in Chemical & Biological Engineering and a B.S. in Chemistry from Colorado State University in 2010. During my time at Colorado State, I had the opportunity join a research group led by Arthur Mayeno and Brad Reisfeld. Working with them inspired me to pursue a graduate degree, and in 2016 I was awarded a Ph.D. in Chemical & Biological Engineering from Princeton University, under the supervision of Mark Brynildsen. Near the end of my Ph.D. I met Jens Nielsen during his visit to give a seminar at Princeton. Soon after, I joined his group in Sweden as a postdoc.

When I'm not in lab, I enjoy cycling, snowboarding, learning Swedish, coding, and traveling.
